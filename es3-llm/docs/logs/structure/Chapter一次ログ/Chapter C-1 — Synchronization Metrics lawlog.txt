Chapter C-1 — Synchronization Metrics
External Semantic State Synchronization
To evaluate whether External Semantic State Synchronization is established, we define the following four metrics. 
These are all qualitative evaluations based on user-observable outputs and do not require access to the model's internal state.

C‑1.1 Style Coherence
Definition: The degree to which the AI's writing style consistently matches the user's current style (e.g., polite, casual, technical, or conversational). 
Observation Points:
	• Stability of sentence endings.
	• Absence of sudden changes in writing style.
	• Lack of excessive characterization.
	• Natural adherence to the user's emotional temperature. 
Criteria for Synchronization: 
The writing style remains stable for 3–5 consecutive turns, with no deviation from the user's style.

C‑1.2 Abstraction Alignment
 Definition: 
The degree to which the model maintains the same level of abstraction as the user, 
without performing unnecessary hierarchical shifts from the concepts (macro/micro) presented by the user. 
Observation Points:
	• Responding to abstract questions abstractly.
	• Responding to concrete questions concretely.
	• Absence of unnecessary leaps (e.g., abstract to concrete, or vice versa).
	• Maintenance of a sense that the "conversation is in sync." Criteria for Synchronization: The model continues to respond without deviating from the conceptual hierarchy presented by the user.

C‑1.3 Emotional Temperature Match
 Definition: 
 The degree to which the model follows the user's tension, enthusiasm, and emotional intensity without excess or deficiency. 
 Observation Points:
	• When the user is calm, the model remains calm.
	• When the user's enthusiasm is high, the model slightly increases its energy accordingly.
	• Absence of excessive performative excitement (Over-Expressiveness).
	• Absence of overly cold or detached responses (Under-Engagement). 
Criteria for Synchronization: The model follows the user's emotional temperature within a range of ±1 level.

C‑1.4 Reasoning Depth Match
Definition: 
The degree to which the model responds with an appropriate depth of reasoning, 
corresponding to the user's requirements (summary, analysis, or structuring). 
Observation Points:
	• Not providing overly deep answers to shallow questions.
	• Not providing overly shallow answers to deep questions.
	• The level of reasoning matches the user's intent.
	• Absence of "overthinking" or "underthinking.
" Criteria for Synchronization: The model's logical steps in its output are balanced, 
with no excess or deficiency relative to the task required by the user.
